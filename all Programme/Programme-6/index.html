Ouarzazate Programme

Early Morning : Departure from Marrakech

• 6:00 AM: Depart early from Marrakech to maximize your time in Ouarzazate. The drive takes about 3.5 to 4 hours (around 200 km), passing through the scenic High Atlas Mountains via the Tizi n’Tichka pass, which offers stunning views.

Mid-Morning : Arrival in Ouarzazate

• 10:30 AM: Arrive in Ouarzazate and start by visiting the famous Kasbah Taourirt, a UNESCO World Heritage site. This 19th-century kasbah was once the residence of the powerful Glaoui family and features impressive earthen architecture and intricate interiors.

Late Morning : Atlas Film Studios

• 11:30 AM - 12:30 PM: Head to the Atlas Film Studios, one of the largest film studios in the world. Take a guided tour to see the film sets and props from famous movies and TV shows like Gladiator, Game of Thrones, and The Mummy. The tour provides a fascinating insight into the film industry in Morocco.

Afternoon : Ait Benhaddou

• 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM: Enjoy a lunch break at a local restaurant in Ouarzazate, where you can try traditional Moroccan dishes such as tagine or couscous.

• 1:30 PM - 3:00 PM: After lunch, drive to Ait Benhaddou, about 30 minutes from Ouarzazate. This iconic ksar (fortified village) is another UNESCO World Heritage site and has been featured in many films and TV series. Explore the labyrinth of mud-brick buildings, climb to the top for panoramic views, and take in the unique architecture and history of this ancient site.

Late Afternoon : Return to Marrakech

• 3:00 PM: Begin your journey back to Marrakech. You’ll retrace your route through the Atlas Mountains, possibly catching the sunset as you descend into the plains.

• 7:00 PM - 8:00 PM: Arrive back in Marrakech, completing your day trip.

Additional Tips :

• The drive through the Tizi n’Tichka pass can be winding, so if you’re prone to motion sickness, plan accordingly.

• Bring snacks, water, and a jacket, as temperatures can vary between Marrakech and Ouarzazate.

• Wear comfortable shoes, as there will be some walking and climbing involved, especially at Ait Benhaddou.

• Consider hiring a private driver or joining a guided tour to make the journey more comfortable and informative.
